# Defining the domain/core of the application**
  
 - What problem is this application solving.


1. **First step is to define the soild structures that are solving this problem**
   
 - Solid structures are entites that have identity, they are the foundation of the application.


2. **From entities we can think about behavior between them as functionalities**
 
  -  Functionalities in any project can generally be divided into two categories:  
    2.1. Business functionalities – The core requirements of the application; what the app is fundamentally meant to do.  
    2.2. Infrastructural functionalities – Technical and supporting elements that exist to serve the business core.
 
 The project should start by resolving the **business functionalities**. These are essentially the requirements of the app. Infrastructural components are    
 designed afterward, bending to the needs of the business core.

# Starting with the business
  

1. **Recognition** – Identify and categorize all functionalities.

  - Business functionalities can be divided into:
     - **Pure business functionalities** 
     - **Utility functionalities**
       - **Security** – Authentication, authorization, encryption, audit trails.  
       - **Monitoring** – Logging, system health tracking, performance metrics.  
       - **Error handling** – Generic or reusable error management for system stability.

     
2. **Process of describing the functionalities by principe of branching**
  
    - The principle of branching is describing what is enabled and what is disabled base on user input or side effect.
    - The functionalities are going to be described as branching system, they are going to be grouped in more general view.
    - From the general view the dependencies can be viewed as a branching system and how those grouped activities work integrated.
3. **Already defined branching points then constrains can be defined for the broad group of functionalities**
 
    - Defining each branch as having transitions, and by it the constrains.

4. **Eventually designing the endpoint itself**
 
   - Having in advance the more general view the endpoint can be defined within the system.

5. **Finding the level at functionalities at which the system needs to be thinked about**
 
     - Starting by the most general states/environments at which the app can be found.
     - Destructing in that environment the set of functionalities.
     - So the system needs to be seen in different levels of view starting from the most broader.

6. **Binding the functionalities with the things they carry**

     - Describing invariants, constains.
     - The system needs to start from non specific technical description to the adapter which is the specific.
     - The adapter the actual implementation needs to be decoupled by the design of the project, so later can be easily changed.

7. **At the right level of functions view the docs we contain uniform style**
  
    - Rigth level of view requirements has a separate folder, with its utilities, infra functionalities, and the actual implementation with the framework   
      specific.

8. **The implementation of the adapter/framework**


    - This needs its own way of structuring that is describing at certain level the specific framework solution to the business logic
